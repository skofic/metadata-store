## [Information section](_info.md)

**[Title](_title.md):** United Nations Regional Group

**[Definition](_definition.md):** The United Nations General Assembly regional group to which a country belongs for electoral and procedural purposes.

**[Description](_description.md)**

The UN General Assembly divides its 193 member states into five regional groups used to allocate seats on UN bodies, coordinate voting, and manage candidatures. The groups are: African Group, Asia-Pacific Group, Eastern European Group, Latin American and Caribbean Group (GRULAC), and Western European and Others Group (WEOG). Group membership does not always follow strict geographic lines — for example, Israel and the United States are in WEOG despite their geographic locations.
