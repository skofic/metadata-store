## [Information section](_info.md)

**[Title](_title.md):** Top-level domains

**[Definition](_definition.md):** Namespace for Internet country-code top-level domain identifiers assigned by IANA.

**[Description](_description.md)**

Country-code top-level domains (ccTLDs) are two-letter Internet domain suffixes delegated to countries and territories by the Internet Assigned Numbers Authority (IANA). They are derived from ISO 3166-1 alpha-2 codes (e.g. `.it` for Italy, `.de` for Germany). Some territories have multiple delegated TLDs; a few historical codes remain in use even after the corresponding country no longer exists.
