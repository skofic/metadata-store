## [Information section](_info.md)

**[Title](_title.md):** Molar flux density

**[Definition](_definition.md):** Quantity kind for the molar flow rate of a substance per unit area.

**[Description](_description.md)**

Molar flux density (also called molar flux or molar conductance per area) is the amount of substance crossing a unit area per unit time. The SI coherent unit is the **mole per square metre per second** ([`unit_MolarFluxDensity_mol-per-m2-per-s`](unit_MolarFluxDensity_mol-per-m2-per-s.md), equal to mol·m⁻²·s⁻¹).

Dimensional analysis (QUDT): `A1E0L-2I0M0H0T-1D0`.

In the EUFGIS database, molar flux density units appear in stomatal and residual leaf conductance measurements. Leaf conductance to water vapour (Gres — residual conductance after stomatal closure) is conventionally expressed in millimoles per square metre per second (mmol/m²/s). A high residual conductance means the leaf continues to lose water at a significant rate even when stomata are fully closed.
