## [Information section](_info.md)

**[Title](_title.md):** Gigabase pair (Gb)

**[Definition](_definition.md):** Unit of sequence length equal to 10⁹ base pairs.

**[Description](_description.md)**

The gigabase pair (Gb) is the appropriate unit for whole-genome sizes. Many economically and ecologically important tree genomes are in the gigabase range: Norway spruce (Picea abies) ≈ 19.6 Gb, Scots pine (Pinus sylvestris) ≈ 22 Gb, pedunculate oak (Quercus robur) ≈ 0.81 Gb. Reporting genome size in Gb is standard in genomics and genome assembly publications.
