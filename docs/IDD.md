## [Information section](_info.md)

**[Title](_title.md):** International Direct Dialing

**[Definition](_definition.md):** Namespace for International Direct Dialing telephone calling codes assigned by the ITU.

**[Description](_description.md)**

International Direct Dialing (IDD) codes, also called country calling codes, are the numeric prefixes dialled before a local telephone number when placing an international call. They are assigned by the International Telecommunication Union (ITU) under the ITU-T E.164 standard. A single country may be assigned multiple calling codes, and the same code may be shared among several countries (e.g. +1 covers the United States, Canada, and many Caribbean nations).
