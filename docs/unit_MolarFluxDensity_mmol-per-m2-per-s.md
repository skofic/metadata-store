## [Information section](_info.md)

**[Title](_title.md):** Millimole per square metre per second (mmol/m²/s)

**[Definition](_definition.md):** Unit of molar flux density equal to 10⁻³ mol/m²/s.

**[Description](_description.md)**

Millimole per square metre per second is the standard unit for stomatal conductance and transpiration flux in plant physiology. In the EUFGIS phenotypic characterisation dataset, residual stomatal conductance (Gres — the minimum conductance at turgor loss) is recorded in this unit. Typical midday values for well-watered trees are 100–400 mmol/m²/s; Gres typically ranges from 1 to 20 mmol/m²/s.
