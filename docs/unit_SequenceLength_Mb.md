## [Information section](_info.md)

**[Title](_title.md):** Megabase pair (Mb)

**[Definition](_definition.md):** Unit of sequence length equal to 10⁶ base pairs.

**[Description](_description.md)**

The megabase pair (Mb) is used for genome assembly scaffold sizes, chromosome lengths, and whole-genome comparisons. Tree genomes typically range from 400 Mb (poplar) to several gigabases (conifers can exceed 20 Gb). Genomic windows for landscape genomics analyses in EUFGIS studies are often defined in Mb.
