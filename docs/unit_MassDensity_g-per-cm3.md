## [Information section](_info.md)

**[Title](_title.md):** Gram per cubic centimetre (g/cm³)

**[Definition](_definition.md):** Unit of mass density equal to 1 000 kg/m³.

**[Description](_description.md)**

Gram per cubic centimetre is the conventional unit for wood density in plant physiology and forest ecology. Wood density (also called wood specific gravity when normalised to water) typically ranges from 0.2 g/cm³ (balsa) to 1.3 g/cm³ (tropical hardwoods). The EUFGIS phenotypic dataset records wood density in this unit. Note that 1 g/cm³ = 1 000 kg/m³ (water density at 4 °C).
